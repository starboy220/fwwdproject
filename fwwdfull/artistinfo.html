About the Artist

Starling Thapa is a renowned Khatra painter from Nepal, known for his intricate brushwork and vibrant depictions of Nepalese culture and mythology. With over two decades of experience, he has become a leading figure in the preservation and evolution of traditional Khatra art.

His journey began in the ancient city of Bhaktapur, where he apprenticed under master painters from the age of 15. After years of rigorous training, he developed a unique style that blends classical techniques with contemporary themes.

The Journey to Success

Growing up in the narrow alleys of Bhaktapur, I was surrounded by ancient temples adorned with exquisite paintings. My grandfather, himself a woodcarver, would tell me stories of the gods and heroes depicted on those walls. At 15, I convinced a local master to take me as an apprentice. For six years, I ground minerals, prepared canvases, and copied the masters' works stroke by stroke.

My breakthrough came in 2012 when my painting "Kumari's Dream" was selected for the Nepal Art Council's annual exhibition. A collector from New York purchased it, and that sale opened doors to international galleries. Today, my works are in private collections across Europe, Asia, and America, but my heart remains in the studios of Bhaktapur, where I continue to train young artists in the dying art of Khatra painting.

For me, success isn't just about recognition—it's about ensuring this ancient art form survives for generations to come.
